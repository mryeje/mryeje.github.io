Module 1: Your Secret Lab - Getting to Know Roblox Studio

Objective: Get comfortable with the Roblox Studio interface and 3D movement. No coding yet!

Mission Briefing

First things first, Agent L. Every master builder needs a secret lab. We're going to get you set up in Roblox Studio and teach you how to move around and use your basic tools. This is like learning where all the blocks are in Mario Maker before you start building.

Lessons:

  1. Downloading Your Lab: Your first task is to get the software. With a grown-up's help, go to the Roblox website and find the "Create" button. Follow the instructions to download and install Roblox Studio. This is your new headquarters!
  2. The Three Big Screens: When you open your lab, you'll see a lot of buttons. Don't worry! We only need to know three things for now:
    • The 3D Viewport: The giant window in the middle. This is your building area!
    • The Explorer: This is your Toy Box. It's a list on the right side that shows every single thing in your game.
    • The Properties Window: This is the Control Panel. When you click on a toy from your Toy Box, this window lets you change everything about it.
  3. Flying and Walking: To move around your lab, use the W, A, S, and D keys, just like in many games. To look around, hold down the right mouse button and move your mouse. You can fly! This is how you'll get a good view of your creations.
  4. Your First Building Block: Let's add something to your empty world. At the top of the screen, find the Home tab and click the button that says Part. A gray brick will appear. Congratulations, you've made your first object!
  5. Project #1: The Rainbow Road: Time for your first project. Add a bunch of Parts to your world. Click on each one and look at the Properties window (your Control Panel). Find the property called BrickColor and click it to change the color. Find the one called Material to change its texture from plastic to wood, metal, or even grass! Try to make a long road with many different colors and materials.
  6. The Most Important Button in the Universe: Click on one of your new parts and press the Play button at the top. Uh oh! Did your part fall out of the sky? That's because it doesn't have gravity turned off. Now, stop the game. Select your part again and find the Anchor button in the Home tab. It looks like an anchor from a ship. Click it. Now press Play again. It stays put! ALWAYS ANCHOR YOUR PLATFORMS! This is the #1 rule for all master builders.

Module 2: The Art of the Obby - Building Your First Jumps

Objective: Use the building tools to create a simple, multi-stage platforming challenge.

Mission Briefing

Okay, Agent L, you know your way around the lab. Now it's time to build the foundation of your obby. We're going to create a series of jumps. Remember, a good obby is like a good joke: it's all about the timing!

Today's Joke: Why did the scarecrow win an award?

... Because he was outstanding in his field!

Lessons:

  1. The Holy Trinity of Tools: At the top of the screen, next to the Anchor button, you will find your three most important tools: Move, Scale, and Rotate.
    • Move: Lets you move parts up, down, left, and right.
    • Scale: Lets you make parts bigger or smaller, longer or shorter.
    • Rotate: Lets you spin parts around.
    Practice using these three tools on a Part until you feel like a pro.
  2. Deleting the Floor: A good obby is dangerous! Let's get rid of that boring floor. In your Explorer window (the Toy Box), find the item named Baseplate. Click on it, and then press the Delete key on your keyboard. Poof! Now if a player falls, they fall into the endless sky!
  3. The Spawn Point: The block you start on is called a SpawnLocation. You can use the Move tool to place it at the beginning of your obby. Make sure the little person icon on it is facing the direction you want the player to go.
  4. Project #2: The Leaping Lizard Gauntlet: Time for your second project. Your mission is to build a sequence of 5 to 7 platforms that a player has to jump across. Use the Scale and Move tools to make some jumps short, some long, some high, and some low. After you place a few platforms, use the Play Here button (it's under the main Play button) to test your course. Are the jumps possible? Are they too easy? Are they too hard? A master builder always tests their work!

Module 3: The Secret Code - Making Things Happen with Scripts

Objective: Write your very first Lua script to make a part interactive.

Mission Briefing

Agent L, you've mastered building. But what if you could give your blocks... superpowers? That's where scripting comes in. It's a secret code that tells your parts what to do. Our first spell will be the "Disappearing Platform" trick!

Today's Riddle: What has to be broken before you can use it?

... An egg! And we're about to "crack" the code!

Lessons:

  1. What's a Script? A script is just a list of instructions for the computer, like a recipe for a cake. The computer is very literal, so it will follow your recipe exactly as you write it. The language for these recipes is called Lua.
  2. Your First Script: In the Explorer, find one of the platforms you made. Click the little + sign next to its name and select Script. A new window will open. It will probably say print("Hello, world!"). This is a tradition for coders, but we don't need it. Delete that line so you have a blank page.
  3. The Recipe for a Fading Trap: We are going to write a script that makes a platform disappear for a few seconds when a player touches it. Type this recipe exactly as you see it. Punctuation and capitalization are very important! 
    local part = script.Parent

local function onTouch(otherPart)
    if otherPart.Parent:FindFirstChild("Humanoid") then
        part.Transparency = 1
        part.CanCollide = false
        wait(3)
        part.Transparency = 0
        part.CanCollide = true
    end
end

part.Touched:Connect(onTouch)
  4. Project #3: The Ghost Bridge: Now for the fun part. Build a long bridge made of 5 or 6 separate parts. Put this same script inside every single part of the bridge. Now when you test it, you'll have to run across before the bridge disappears behind you!

Table 2: Agent L's Secret Spellbook (Your First Lua Commands)

The Spell (What to Type) What it Does (The Magic Effect) Secret Code Notes
local part = script.Parent Creates a variable (a nickname) called part that holds the object the script is inside. script.Parent literally means "the parent of this script." It's a super useful spell!
part.Transparency = 1 Makes the part completely invisible. 1 is invisible. 0 is solid. 0.5 is see-through like a ghost!
part.CanCollide = false Lets players walk right through the part. false means OFF (no colliding). true means ON (you can stand on it).
wait(3) Pauses the script for a number of seconds. You can change the number in the parentheses to wait for a longer or shorter time.
part.Touched:Connect(onTouch) This is a powerful trigger! It runs a function whenever the part is touched by something. This is how you make traps and buttons work!

Module 4: Level Up! - Creating Killer Obstacles

Objective: Learn to create classic obby obstacles like kill bricks and conveyors.

Mission Briefing

Every great obby needs great traps. It's time to add some real danger to your course, Agent L. We'll create "lava bricks" that reset the player and speedy conveyors that try to push them off!

Today's Joke: What do you call a cow that can't moo?

... A milk dud!

Lessons:

  1. The Insta-Reset Brick (Kill Brick): This is one of the most popular traps. Make a new Part, color it red like lava, and Anchor it. Now, add a new script to it with this recipe: 
    local killBrick = script.Parent

local function onTouch(otherPart)
    local partParent = otherPart.Parent
    local humanoid = partParent:FindFirstChild("Humanoid")

    if humanoid then
        humanoid.Health = 0
    end
end

killBrick.Touched:Connect(onTouch)
  2. The Pushy Platform (Conveyor): This one is even easier and doesn't need a script to start! Add a new Part and make it long like a treadmill. In the Properties window, find the property called Velocity. This is the speed and direction the part is moving. Try typing 10 into the first number (the X value). Now when you play, the part will push you! You can change the numbers to make it push players faster, slower, or in different directions. You can even make them go up!
  3. Project #4: The Gauntlet of Doom: Your mission is to combine everything you have learned. Create a new section of your obby that has:
    • A few normal jumps.
    • A disappearing "Ghost Bridge."
    • A floor of red "Kill Bricks" you have to jump over.
    • A final conveyor belt that tries to push you off the finish line.
    This will be your most challenging and awesome section yet!

Module 5: Going Public! - Sharing Your Game with the World

Objective: Learn how to save, configure, and publish the game.

Mission Briefing

Your obby is complete, Agent L. The final step is to unleash it upon the world! It's time to publish your game so your friends (and future fans) can play it.

Today's Riddle: It belongs to you, but other people use it more than you do. What is it?

... Your name! And now, everyone's going to know the name of your awesome game!

Lessons:

  1. Saving Your Masterpiece: You should save your work often! In the top-left corner, click File.
    • Save to File saves a copy on your computer.
    • Save to Roblox saves it to your online Roblox account. This is what we want to do before we publish.
  2. Game Settings 101: After you save to Roblox, go to the Home tab and click on Game Settings. Here you can give your game a cool name and write a description telling people what it's about.
  3. Flipping the Switch: Making it Public: In the same Game Settings menu, go to the Permissions tab. You will see an option for Public. Click it! Now your game is live on the Roblox platform. You can go to your game's page on the Roblox website and copy the link to share with your friends so they can try to beat your course.
  4. Mission Accomplished! Congratulations, Agent L! You have completed your training and published your very first game. You went from a Master Builder to a Master Coder. Your skills are now legendary. But this is just the beginning. Keep experimenting, keep building, and keep trying new things. The Roblox world is yours to create!
