Texas Hold'em Mastery: A Course Syllabus

**Course Code:** POKER-401 | **Semester:** Fall 2025 | **Instructor:** AI Poker Professor

This course provides intermediate-to-advanced online Texas Hold'em tournament players with a structured, mastery-driven framework for continuous improvement. It integrates cutting-edge strategic concepts with robust mental game techniques, specifically tailored for individuals with ADHD, a preference for heuristic thinking, and a motivation rooted in deep understanding rather than purely financial gain. Students will develop personalized study systems, cultivate an adaptive "calculated maniac" playing style, and foster profound emotional resilience to navigate the inherent randomness and psychological demands of the game.

Learning Objectives

  • Design and implement a personalized, ADHD-friendly poker study system utilizing spaced repetition and knowledge management tools.
  • Articulate and apply core preflop and postflop heuristics for online tournament play without a HUD.
  • Differentiate between Game Theory Optimal (GTO) and exploitative strategies, and fluidly adapt their approach based on opponent tendencies.
  • Cultivate a "calculated maniac" table image, leveraging strategic aggression for maximum tournament equity.
  • Identify, analyze, and mitigate personal emotional leaks, including tilt and entitlement.
  • Apply Stoic philosophy and mindfulness principles to accept variance, manage ego, and maintain decision quality irrespective of outcome.
  • Utilize advanced poker software and resources for deep analysis and practice.

Module 1: Laying the Foundation ‚Äì The Mastery Mindset & Personal Systems

Week 1: Understanding Your Learning Profile & Setting Goals

Topic: Introduction to the mastery-based approach; self-assessment of learning style (ADHD, heuristic preference); defining personal poker goals beyond money.

Learning Objectives: Understand the course philosophy; identify individual cognitive strengths and challenges; set clear, mastery-driven objectives.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Introduction: The Evolving Landscape of Online Poker Mastery" from the "Optimizing Online Texas Hold'em Performance" report.
  • *Optional:* Excerpts from "Peak: Secrets from the New Science of Expertise" by Anders Ericsson (focus on deliberate practice).

Exercises:

  • Complete a "Player Archetype Profile" self-assessment (reflect on the traits presented in the web app).
  • Journaling: Write down your core motivations for poker and long-term mastery goals.

Week 2: Building Your ADHD-Friendly Study System

Topic: Environmental control; Pomodoro Technique & time blocking; micro-tasks and task chunking; physical activity and self-care for focus.

Learning Objectives: Implement foundational study habits; create a personalized weekly study schedule.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Personal Study Systems & Journeys: Tailored Approaches for Cognitive Diversity" from the "Optimizing Online Texas Hold'em Performance" report.
  • Article: "Studying Poker: Your Weekly Study Guide In 2025" (SplitSuit).

Exercises:

  • Design your "Weekly Study Planner" (using the interactive planner concept from the web app as inspiration).
  • Practice the Pomodoro Technique for at least 3 study sessions.

Module 2: Strategic Foundations ‚Äì Preflop & Postflop Heuristics

Week 3: Preflop Heuristics for Tournament Play

Topic: Developing simple, actionable rules of thumb for opening, calling, and 3-betting ranges in various positions. Adjusting for stack depth.

Learning Objectives: Apply fundamental preflop heuristics; understand the importance of position.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Simple Preflop Heuristics (Rules of Thumb)" from "The Calculated Maniac Playbook" section of the web app.
  • Video: "8 ADVANCED Poker Tips For PREFLOP" (Splitsuit on YouTube).

Exercises:

  • Create 5 simple preflop heuristics for your game, focusing on different positions (UTG, BTN, SB).
  • Review 10 of your own played hands, analyzing your preflop decisions against your new heuristics.

Week 4: Postflop Rules of Thumb & Basic Exploits (No HUD)

Topic: Developing simple postflop rules for continuation betting, check-raising, and value betting. Identifying common opponent leaks without a HUD.

Learning Objectives: Apply basic postflop heuristics; begin recognizing exploitable tendencies.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Strategic Postflop Rules" and "Exploiting without a HUD" from "The Calculated Maniac Playbook" section of the web app.

Exercises:

  • Create 3 postflop rules of thumb based on common opponent types (e.g., calling station, tight-passive player).
  • During your next play session, take mental notes on 3 opponents' obvious tendencies.

Module 3: Advanced Strategy ‚Äì The Calculated Maniac & GTO Integration

Week 5: The "Calculated Maniac" Image & Strategic Aggression

Topic: Cultivating a deceptive, aggressive table image; understanding the psychology behind it; balancing aggression with control.

Learning Objectives: Understand how to build and leverage a "calculated maniac" image in tournaments.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Cultivating the Aggressive Image" from "The Calculated Maniac Playbook" section of the web app.
  • Article: "How to Exploit Your Table Image in Poker" (PokerListings).

Exercises:

  • Plan 2 specific spots in your next session where you will deliberately cultivate your "maniac" image (e.g., showing down a bluff, raising a wide range from a specific position).
  • Self-reflection: How do you think opponents perceive your play?

Week 6: GTO Fundamentals & The GTO vs. Exploitative Spectrum

Topic: Introduction to Game Theory Optimal (GTO) concepts; understanding GTO as an unexploitable baseline; the dynamic relationship between GTO and exploitative play.

Learning Objectives: Grasp the core principles of GTO; understand when to lean towards GTO vs. exploitative play.

Readings/Resources:

  • "The GTO vs. Exploitative Spectrum" section of the web app.
  • Article: "Exploitative vs GTO - Why you need to learn both to be a decent player." (r/poker Reddit thread).

Exercises:

  • Use the GTO vs. Exploitative slider concept from the web app and write down your understanding of each extreme and the balanced approach.
  • Practice a simple GTO concept (e.g., minimum defense frequency) using a solver tool (e.g., Deepsolver) for 15-20 minutes.

Module 4: Mental Game Mastery ‚Äì Ego, Variance, and Resilience

Week 7: Conquering Tilt & Emotional Leaks

Topic: Identifying tilt triggers; practical strategies for managing emotional responses (breaks, bankroll management, self-care).

Learning Objectives: Recognize and actively combat tilt; implement immediate "tilt-breaking" strategies.

Readings/Resources:

  • "The Tilt-Breaker Flowchart" section of the web app.
  • Article: "7 Tips to Overcome Bad Beats in Poker" (Conscious Poker).
  • Book: "The Mental Game of Poker" by Jared Tendler (Chapters on Tilt).

Exercises:

  • Journaling: Document your last 3 tilt experiences, identifying triggers and what you could have done differently.
  • Practice the "Tilt-Breaker Flowchart" mentally during your next session if tilt arises.

Week 8: Embracing Randomness & Taming the Ego

Topic: The philosophy of variance; distinguishing decision quality from outcome; applying Stoic principles; understanding and overcoming "entitlement tilt."

Learning Objectives: Develop a philosophical acceptance of randomness; detach ego from results; focus on process over outcome.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Philosophical Anchors" (Stoicism & Ego) section of the web app.
  • Article: "Why Using Stoic Philosophy Will Skyrocket Your Poker Winnings" (BlackRain79).

Exercises:

  • Reflect on a recent bad beat: Did you judge the outcome or the decision? Reframe it through a Stoic lens.
  • Practice mindfulness for 5-10 minutes daily, focusing on observing thoughts without judgment.

Module 5: Advanced Tools & Continuous Improvement

Week 9: Leveraging Advanced Study Tools

Topic: Deep dive into Notion for advanced knowledge management; Anki for complex concept memorization; utilizing solvers for in-depth analysis.

Learning Objectives: Master the use of Notion and Anki for personalized study; effectively use solvers for specific hand analysis.

Readings/Resources:

  • "Tools of the Trade" (Notion, Anki, Spreadsheets) section of the web app.
  • "Digital Tools for Structured Practice" from the "Optimizing Online Texas Hold'em Performance" report.
  • Notion Poker Player's Dashboard template.
  • Anki Spaced Repetition Software.
  • toanki.net (for converting Notion notes to Anki).

Exercises:

  • Set up a Notion database for your poker study notes and integrate it with Anki (if not already done).
  • Analyze 3 complex hands using a solver (e.g., Deepsolver, Simple Postflop), focusing on understanding the underlying GTO principles.

Week 10: Simulating Pressure & Sustaining the Journey

Topic: Simulating live poker pressure online; long-term commitment to mastery; adapting to evolving game dynamics; preventing burnout.

Learning Objectives: Develop strategies for simulating pressure; create a long-term plan for sustained improvement.

Readings/Resources:

  • Article: "Why Playing Smaller Field MTTs is Good For Your Mental Game" (Jared Tendler).
  • Self-reflection on sustaining motivation and avoiding burnout.

Exercises:

  • Identify one way you can simulate pressure in your online play (e.g., playing higher stakes for a short, focused period, or setting specific performance goals for a session).
  • Develop a 3-month personal poker study roadmap, outlining key areas of focus.

Assessment

  • Weekly Journaling/Reflection (40%): Consistent documentation of study, play, and mental game insights.
  • Hand History Analysis (30%): Submission of 3 detailed hand analyses demonstrating application of strategic concepts and mental game principles.
  • Personalized Study System Design (20%): Presentation of a comprehensive, tailored study system (e.g., Notion setup, Anki deck structure).
  • Final Reflection Essay (10%): A philosophical reflection on personal growth, ego management, and acceptance of randomness in your poker journey.

Required Software/Tools

  • Notion (free personal plan sufficient).
